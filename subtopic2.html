CGI and 3D Animation Technology

URL: Pixar – Creative Legacy
Author: Pixar Animation Studios
Abstract: This source describes Pixar’s pioneering role in CGI development, beginning with early experiments in computer rendering and leading to the first fully CGI feature film, Toy Story. It highlights innovations such as advanced modeling, shading algorithms, and rendering engines that allowed Pixar to create lifelike characters and environments. The site explains how CGI opened new storytelling possibilities by allowing complete control over lighting, physics, and world-building—ultimately redefining modern animation and influencing film studios worldwide.
URL: Smithsonian Magazine – How Computers Changed Art
Author: Smithsonian Magazine
Abstract: This article examines how computers transformed the creative process, especially in animation and film. It explains how digital tools replaced physical effects and expanded visual possibilities with photorealistic textures, 3D simulations, and dynamic movement. The piece highlights how artists began collaborating with programmers to build digital pipelines that made animation faster and more flexible. The article emphasizes how CGI elevated visual storytelling and dramatically influenced modern filmmaking standards.
