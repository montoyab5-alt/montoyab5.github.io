Digital Art Tools and Software

URL: Adobe – History of Digital Art Tools
Author: Adobe Inc.
Abstract: This source explains how digital art tools evolved from simple bitmap editors to powerful creative platforms like Photoshop, Illustrator, and Fresco. Adobe highlights how features such as layers, digital brushes, and high-resolution canvases changed artistic workflows. The article outlines how graphic tablets and stylus technology made digital art more intuitive, allowing artists to work with greater precision and experimentation. It stresses how digital tools have become essential for concept art, illustration, animation, and professional design industries.
URL: Computer History Museum – Evolution of Digital Art
Author: Computer History Museum
Abstract: This article reviews the history of digital art, beginning with early computer-generated images and programming-based artwork. It explains how technological improvements—such as GPUs, digital tablets, and advanced graphic software—expanded the abilities of artists to create complex visuals. The museum emphasizes how digital tools democratized art-making, allowing both professionals and newcomers to produce high-quality work. The evolution of hardware and software paved the way for modern multimedia and interactive digital art forms.
